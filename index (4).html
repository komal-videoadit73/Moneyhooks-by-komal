About Me

Hi, I'm Komal — welcome to my little corner of the internet.

I didn't grow up learning about money in school, and I don't think most of us did. I picked things up slowly — through mistakes, through questions I was embarrassed to ask, and through a lot of trial and error with my own budget. Money Hooks by Komal is the resource I wish I'd had when I was starting out.

I started this project because I kept meeting people — students figuring out their first allowance, parents trying to teach their kids about saving, families juggling a tight monthly budget — who all wanted the same thing: someone to explain money in plain language, without judgment and without selling them something.

That's what I try to do here. Every lesson, video and tip is written the way I'd explain it to a friend over coffee — practical, honest, and easy to actually use. If it helps even one person feel a little more in control of their money, this project has done its job.

Money Saving Tips

Small habits that add up

01

Track your expenses

Write down everything you spend for two weeks. You can't fix what you can't see.

02

Create a monthly budget

Give every rupee a job before the month starts, so your money has direction.

03

Save before spending

Move savings out the moment you're paid, so what's left is what you can spend.

04

Avoid impulse buying

Wait 24 hours before any non-essential purchase. Most urges fade on their own.

05

Compare prices before shopping

A few minutes of comparing can save more than an hour of extra work would earn.

06

Build an emergency fund

Even a small cushion keeps one bad month from turning into a financial crisis.
